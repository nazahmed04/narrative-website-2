Dining

get a true taste of Italy

top 5 dining spots in rome

gianno restaurant - ££££

“What we call tradition is yesterday's innovation.” - Chef Ciccio Sultano of Giano. The legacy of Sultano began in a Michelin-starred restaurant in Sicily, with a richness of Italian tradition in his bold flavours. In 2022, Giano opened in the W Hotel in Rome. The cuisine remains fresh and grounded from a selection of pasta to a grilled ribeye.

trapizzino - £

Located in Testaccip, this pizza joint has been sharing divine street snacks since 2009. Chef Stefano Callegaro’s simple signature Trapizzino consists of fluffy pizza biianca stuffed with classic Italian and Roman dishes. This is a great affordable choice to delve into the traditional local cuisine.

supplì roma - £

Named for its specialty, this takeaway spot in Trastavere sells some of the best value Roman snacks. Following the Rome tradition, gnocchi is always on the menu every Thursday. Be rewarded with pasta, cannelloni, pizza, and of course suppli when you take your visit to this bustling spot.

salumeria roscioli - £££

Here you will be greeted at the long deli counter, with an entire menu dedicated to salt-cured anchovies and a vast assortment of cheeses. The rest of the Rossioli experience is ready for you to explore the rich Italian culture. The extensive menu of Roscioli's, hosting many celebrated dishes is paired perfectly with the deep wine cellar.

pinsere - £

A short walk away from Termini station, lies Pinsere. Unlike the pizza-by-the-slice joints that surround Rome, here pinsa is served, a mini pizza for you to enjoy with a huge range of toppings. Pinsere utilises local ingredients for the classic Margherita to creative combinations of your choice.

"Buon appetito!"
