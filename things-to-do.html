Things to do

immerse into breathtaking landscapes, captivating culture, and world-class landmarks.

explore rome

arts

Roman art is composed by iconic sculptures, paintings, and mosaics. Each piece depicts historical, religious, and mythological themes as well as everyday life in the people of Rome.

culture

The eclectic mix of historic and modern culture defines Rome as the Eternal City.

nature

Endowed with a sheer class and variety of urban parks, rivers, and iconic hills, Rome is one of Europe's greenest cities.

landmarks

pantheon

Originally constructed as a temple, the pantheon is the most preserved monument in Ancient Rome, built in 125AD. It's giant dome remains the largest in the world and the top is open allowing beautiful sun rays to light up the interior for you to see.

trevi fountain

The Trevi fountain originates back to 19BC and is located in the Trevi district of Rome. The intricate Baroque artwork makes it an unmissable sight in the city, whether under the Italian daylight or warmly lit up at night.

colloseum

Built during the Roman Empire, the Colosseum is the largest amphitheatre in the world. It stands high to represent Rome's architectural beauty and power dating back to the ancient gladiator battles that were hosted here.

villa borghese

The Villa Borghese is considered Rome's green lung. The landscape garden contains a number of artworks, lakes, fountains, and museums. One of which being Galleria Borghese, the home to a beautiful and significant collection of art.

castel sant'angelo

Castel Sant'Angelo is known as the castle of the Holy Angel. The 6 story building with 58 rooms is located on the Tiber river; connected by Ponte Sant'Angelo, the bridge of angels. Experience the turbulent history by taking a visit.

vatican museum

The Vatican Museum holds the essence of Roman. Baroque, and Rennaisance architecture and art. It is home to great masterpieces, including Michelangelo's Fresco. As Vatican is considered a holy city for Catholics, significant cultural references are on the walls of this museum.

hidden gems

quartiere coppedè

Nestled within the North of Rome, is the secret and charming neighbourhood of Quartiere Coppedè. With an outdoor chandelier, cherubs, archways, and animals.

tiber island

Hosting a Baroque cathedral and 16th century hospital, the sacred island remains associated with healing. The first thing to notice is its shape and from here you can admire the bridges and nature of Rome.

park of the aqueducts

Away from crowds, the Aqueduct Park contrasts between the beautiful nature of Rome and its medieval history. Explore the ancient architecture and wildflowers for a relaxed escape.

palazzo doria pamphilj

The palace contains more than just paintings, capturing the breathtaking era of Rennaisance art and culture. With an immersive audio guide you'll get the full experience.

pyramid of cestius

The mysterious Pyramid of Cestius was build around 2000 years ago as a mausoleum. It presents the great influence of ancient Egyptian culture on the Roman Empire.

largo di torre argentina

The archaeological site is the theatre of a momentous historical event; when Julius Caesarmet his fate on the Ides of March, 44BC. The impressive ancient Roman ruins are a way to enrich your trip with history.
