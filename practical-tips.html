useful information

travel safety

There are currently no Covid-19 restrictions in Rome and the entirety of Italy. Travelers from any country are allowed to enter Italy without any Covid checks on entry. Mask mandates have also been removed however individual airlines can still enforce mask requirements; it is recommended to check this before boarding. If you test positive during your stay, a quarantine period of 5 days followed by a negative test is obligated. click here for more information

passports and visas

To travel to Rome, you must obtain a valid passport and you can stay in the country of Italy for up to 90 days without a visa. To extend your stay for any reason, you will need to meet the Italian government’s entry requirements and may need a type of visa and/or work permit.

transport

Rome’s public transport system is varied and efficient. The metro has 3 lines that cross the city, with stops near the city’s top attractions. There are also 3 urban railway lines called Ferrovie Urbane. These compliment the metro with the same tickets and fares. Buses are more popular, and tickets can be bought in any metro station, news stand, or convenience stores; travel cards are also available. Rome has six tram lines, although they don’t reach the city centre.

Italy's emergency phone number

the single number to call is 112. This number is free to dial, and the operator will put you through to the most suitable service that will help you.
